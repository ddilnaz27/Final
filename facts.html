Gallery of Facts

Istanbul

Istanbul was named partially a Unesco world heritage center.

Many people think that it's Turkey's capital (It's Ankara).

Cappadocia

It's undeniably one of the major Turkey destinations mostly known for hot air ballooning.

Ephesus

This ancient city of Ephesus was constructed in the 10th century BC and housed over 300,000 people during its time.

Oludeniz

Located in the district of Fethiye, Oludeniz (also known as Blue Lagoon in Turkish) is a small beach resort town perfect for a romantic getaway.

Pamukkale

Famously known for the mineral-rich thermal waters flowing down the white travertine terraces on a nearby hillside (translated as “cotton castle” in English).

Side

Side is a resort town with a touch of ancient history.

Side’s theatre was the largest in ancient Pamphylia with a sitting capacity of 15,000 people.

Mount Ararat

Mount Ararat is the mountain where Noah’s ark which is talked about in the bible landed.

Although there is a bit of controversy about it, this theory has been widely accepted by Christians and some archeologists around the world.

Halicarnassus Mausoleum

One of the "7 wonders of the ancient world" located in Turkey.

The second is Temple of Artemis at Ephesus.

Turkish Delight

Turkish delight is the British name for a sweet called lokum.

The original phrase, rahatü'l-hulkum, which means “giving rest to the throat.”

Istanbul

Istanbul(Constantinople) was a capital of Rome Empire.

Must-see tourist attractions in Istanbul include the Blue Mosque, Hagia Sophia, Pera Museum, the Grandbazaar, etc.

The Blue Mosque

One of the most breathtaking mosques in Turkey is the Sultanahmet Mosque, popularly known as the ‘Blue Mosque’ in Istanbul.

There are 82,693 mosques in Turkey.

Hazelnut

Turkey supplies approximately 75% of the world’s production of hazelnuts and is thus one of the pillars of the Turkish export market.
